What is my favorite game?

This could be a simple sentence, but I really like this game. It is called “Risk of Rain 2.” It is a fast-paced roguelike shooter where a survivor crashes on a planet that was not there prior. The first thing you see when you load up the game is two of the unlockable survivors mentioning that there was a familiar signal from the planet. These survivors explore the alien planet and fight through waves of enemies while collecting items that make the survivor stronger. The main goal of the game is to find the spaceship that is emitting this signal. You survive through increasingly difficult stages, activating teleporters, defeating unique bosses, all while the game gets harder the longer you stay. With the stages and items being different every time you press start, every playthrough is a little different.

Survivors

Commando

Commando is the easily the most beginner-friendly survivor in the game. He is the first survivor a player could play. He uses dual pistols to deal consistent damage and has a reliable kit that focuses on balancing mobility and pressure. His abilities allow him to rapidly fire, roll, and shoot energy blasts that have a similar effect as shotgun pellets. Even though Commando seems basic compared to the rest of the survivor roster, he is fast enough to get out of trouble and shoots hard enough to kill any enemy that approaches.

Commando Wiki

Captain

Captain is a very powerful survivor who feels more like a heavy weapons specialist than a fast fighter. He uses a shotgun that can be charged for a tighter spread. It hits hard close-mid range, and one of his biggest strengths lies in his utility orbital support. Captain can call down beacons that help with healing, stunning enemies, or giving extra support in damage or money. He is strong in both solo play and team play.

Captain Wiki

Artificer

Artificer is a high-damage survivor built around burst damage. She uses fire, ice, and lightning style abilities to hit enemies hard, wipe out groups, or deal heavy damage to bosses when her skills are timed correctly. Unlike some survivors who rely on basic attacks, Artificer depends on cooldowns, which rewards good timing and positioning. Her passive ability lets her hover and slow her fall tremendously.
